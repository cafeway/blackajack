    Blackjack, also known as 21, is one of the world's most popular card games. Its roots can be traced back to several 17th and 18th century French and Spanish games. The game was first introduced in the early nineteenth century in the United States, where it quickly gained popularity. Casinos and gambling houses used to give bonuses to players who held an Ace of Spades and a black Jack, hence the name "Blackjack."
